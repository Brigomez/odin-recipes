Kung Pao Chicken

Because kung pao chicken is so well-liked in the United States, one might be tempted to think of it as a Chinese-American dish. However, this fiery stir-fried chicken is a traditional Sichuan dish.

Although there are many different ways to prepare it, this dish has been around since the late 1800s. Fundamentally, Kung Pao chicken is just a straightforward dish made of chopped up chicken breasts stir-fried in "Kung Pao sauce," which is mainly made up of vinegar, soy sauce, and a small amount of sugar.

Ingredients

  • Boneless skinless chicken breast
  • Soy sauce (or tamari)
  • Vinegar (rice vinegar or sherry vinegar)
  • Cornstarch
  • Granulated sugar
  • Toasted sesame and vegetable oil
  • Red & green bell peppers
  • Green onions
  • Cashews
  • Dried out chili peppers
  • Fresh garlic & ginger

Steps to make the Kung Pao Chicken

  1. Kung Pao sauce: To make the Kung Pao sauce, combine the vinegar, sugar, and soy sauce in a bowl.
  2. Prep the chicken: Slice the skinless, boneless chicken breast into even strips using a sharp knife. If your chicken is a little frozen, this will be a little simpler to accomplish. After that, coat it with a little soy sauce and cornstarch.
  3. Cook the chicken until crispy: Work in batches to avoid packing the pan and stir-fry the chicken pieces in sesame oil until they are cooked through and beautifully crispy, using a large, heavy skillet or a wok if you have one.
  4. Stir-Fry: Add the bell peppers and stir-fry until they start to soften. Next, incorporate the dried chili peppers, cashews, ginger, garlic, and scallions. After a minute or two of stir-frying, add the cooked chicken and Kung Pao sauce.
  5. Simmer: Give the flavors several more minutes to combine over the heat. You want the Kung Pao sauce to coat the chicken and vegetables thoroughly, so it will thicken slightly.
  6. Serve!: Serve this with hot bowls of brown rice or white rice.

Enjoy!
